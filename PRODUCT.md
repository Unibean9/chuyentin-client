# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Chuyen Tin serves Vietnamese parents and grade 9 students preparing for grade 10 specialized informatics entrance exams, HSG informatics contests, and selective school programs. Parents need a clear, credible path before trusting a course with their child's exam year. Students need structured practice, strong fundamentals, and a weekly sense of what to improve next.

## Product Purpose

Chuyen Tin introduces a focused learning and exam-prep platform for specialized informatics. It helps students build algorithmic thinking, practice contest-style problems, work through school-specific exam patterns, and track readiness without relying on vague promises. Success means parents understand the learning path, students know what to do each week, and the brand earns trust before asking for contact.

## Brand Personality

Clear, serious, and encouraging. The brand should feel like a capable teacher explaining a difficult exam path in plain language: confident without exaggeration, rigorous without pressure, warm without becoming childish.

## Anti-references

Avoid colorful kid-style education pages, cartoonish illustrations, loud gradients, inflated pass-rate claims, "100% guaranteed admission" language, and generic edtech templates full of empty metrics. The page should not feel like a game, a motivational poster, or a brochure that overpromises outcomes.

## Design Principles

- Tell the truth before selling: name the work, the uncertainty, and the support structure clearly.
- Make the path visible: show how fundamentals, practice, feedback, and mock exams connect.
- Build parental trust through specificity: explain what parents can observe, not just what the course claims.
- Keep ambition readable: use motion and polish to support comprehension, never to distract.
- Treat informatics as thinking training: emphasize problem solving, algorithmic habits, and exam discipline.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast and readable Vietnamese typography on bright surfaces. Support reduced motion for parents and students who prefer a calmer experience. Avoid relying on color alone to communicate state. Keep copy direct, scannable, and understandable for both technical and non-technical readers.
